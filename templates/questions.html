FAQ's and Help

Q. How do I schedule an appointment?

On your patient portal, look for the section titled
Appointment Information, and click on Schedule
Appointment. From there you will be able to enter
in the necessary information.

Q. How do I update my health conditions?

On your patient portal, look for the section titled
Personal Information, and click on Update Heath Conditions.
From there you will be able to enter in the necessary information.

Q. How do I update my account information?

On your patient portal, look for the section titled
Personal Information, and click on Update Account Information.
From there you will be able to enter in the necessary information.

Q. How do I send an alert?

The Emergency Alert button is located on the right hand
side of your patient portal. Click Emergency Heart Aid Alert,
and from there you can enter in comments and submit the alert.

Q. What information should I bring with me to the doctor visit?

You should bring insurance cards, medical records,
as well as names and phone numbers of other doctors.

Q. How long must a physician keep medical records?

Physicians must keep patient records for six years after the last visit.
Records for children are kept for one year after the childs 18th birthday.

Q. What is your appointment policy?

All doctos must work by appointment only. Unfortunatly,
occasionally appointments require longer time frames than scheduled.
When a situation like this happens, we appreciate your patience.
A sincere attempt is made to stick to the schedile as much as possible.

Q. What should I do if I'm ill?

Our doctors are available 24 hours a day. If you are
feeling sick we ask that you schedule an appointment
during regular office hours. If the situation is urgent please
submit an emergency alert.

Q. What should I do if I miss my appointment?

If you miss your appointment, please reschedule for a time
that better fits your scheudle. For future referance, if
you are unable to make your appointment please cancel it on your
patient portal ahead of time.

Q. Can I get my lab results over the phone?

Yes, but only the doctor is allowed to release the results,
so you might have to wait until a doctor is available.

Q. What are common symptoms of the flu?

People who have the flu often feel some or all of these symptoms.
Fever, cough, sore throat, runny or stuffy nose, muscle or body aches,
headaches, fatgue, possible vomiting and diarrhea

Q. How do I know if I have a fever?

If your temperature is about 100.4 F or higher you most likey have a fever.
Common symptoms are dry mouth, thirst, headache, fatigue, dry skin, and constipation.
